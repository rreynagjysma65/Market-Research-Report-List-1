<p><p>The large-scale HVAC system market comprises two main types: decentralized and centralized HVAC systems. Decentralized systems use individual units for heating and cooling in specific areas, offering flexibility and reduced energy costs for smaller zones. In contrast, centralized HVAC systems rely on a single, large-unit installation to regulate temperature across an entire building or facility, ensuring uniform climate control and ease of maintenance. Both systems serve diverse applications, influenced by building size, energy efficiency, and operational requirements.</p></p>
