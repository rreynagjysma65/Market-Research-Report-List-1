<p><p>Corrosion inhibitors for water treatments are substances designed to prevent or reduce the corrosion of metal surfaces in water systems. The market is categorized into organic and inorganic types. Organic inhibitors often contain compounds like amines and phosphonates, providing effective protection through adsorption on metal surfaces. In contrast, inorganic inhibitors include materials such as phosphates and chromates, which form protective films or scales. Both types play crucial roles in maintaining the integrity of water distribution systems and reducing maintenance costs.</p></p>
