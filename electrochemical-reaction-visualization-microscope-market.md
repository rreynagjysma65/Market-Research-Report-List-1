<p><p>The Electrochemical Reaction Visualization Microscope market is primarily categorized into two types: based on Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) and based on Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM). AFM microscopes utilize a mechanical probe to scan surfaces at the atomic level, providing detailed topographical information and electrochemical characteristics. In contrast, STM microscopes leverage quantum tunneling principles to visualize conductive surfaces at atomic resolutions, enabling insights into electron density and local electrochemical processes. Both types play crucial roles in advanced material and nanotechnology research.</p></p>
