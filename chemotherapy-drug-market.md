<p><ul><li>Antimetabolites</li><li>Plant Alkaloids</li><li>Alkylating Agents</li><li>Antitumor Antibiotics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The chemotherapy drug market is categorized into several key types, including antimetabolites, plant alkaloids, alkylating agents, and antitumor antibiotics. Antimetabolites interfere with DNA and RNA synthesis, disrupting cancer cell growth. Plant alkaloids, derived from plants, inhibit cell division by targeting the mitotic spindle. Alkylating agents work by directly damaging DNA, preventing cancer cell reproduction. Antitumor antibiotics inhibit various cellular processes, leading to cell death. These diverse classes of drugs offer multiple treatment pathways for various cancer types.</p></p>
