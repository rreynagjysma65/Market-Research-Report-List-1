<p><p>The laboratory disposable glove market encompasses three primary types: latex gloves, rubber gloves, and plastic gloves. Latex gloves offer excellent dexterity and tactile sensitivity, making them ideal for lab tasks, but can cause allergies in some users. Rubber gloves are known for their durability and chemical resistance, suitable for handling hazardous materials. Plastic gloves, typically made from polyethylene, provide a cost-effective barrier for low-risk tasks but offer limited tactile sensitivity. Each type serves specific applications within laboratory environments.</p></p>
